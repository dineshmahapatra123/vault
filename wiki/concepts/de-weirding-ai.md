# De-weirding AI

## Definition
The corporate and managerial impulse to normalize generative AI by treating it as standard enterprise software—fitting it into existing processes, assigning it KPIs, and handing its management to IT departments to minimize risk.

## Why It Matters
When organizations "de-weird" AI, they squander its transformative potential. It turns a revolutionary technology into just another wave of office automation, resulting in "workslop" (marginal productivity gains via low-value AI-generated content) rather than genuine innovation.

## Key Arguments
- **Normalization Failure**: Normal technology gets normal rollout plans (e.g., 90% usage targets), but AI isn't normal.
- **Category Error**: Handing control to IT departments whose core mission is risk elimination prevents the necessary "wild experimentation" AI requires.
- **Information Gap**: When firms de-weird AI, employees hide their real (and often most innovative) AI usage to avoid punishment or captured productivity gains.

## Examples
- Using a powerful LLM primarily to transcribe meetings or generate dozens of extra memos.
- Treating a "mysterious alien artefact" as a paperweight because its full potential is too risky to explore.
