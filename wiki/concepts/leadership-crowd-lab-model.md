# Leadership-Crowd-Lab Model

## Definition
Ethan Mollick’s three-part framework for effective organizational AI adoption, designed to resist "de-weirding" and lean into augmentation.

## Why It Matters
Traditional top-down or IT-led software rollouts fail to capture the emergent value of generative AI. This model distributes the responsibility for AI strategy, experimentation, and technical development across three distinct but linked groups.

## Components
- **Leadership**: The CEO and senior managers must set the vision, create incentives for safe experimentation, and use the systems themselves. They cannot delegate AI strategy to middle management.
- **Crowd**: The company’s employees. Since AI is most effective in the hands of experts, the "crowd" finds the best use cases through wild, bottom-up experimentation.
- **Lab**: A dedicated team of technical and non-technical employees working full-time on AI to push boundaries, develop new workflows, and feed discoveries back into the "crowd."

## Examples
- A "Lab" discovering that a specific prompting technique saves 4 hours a day for accountants, and then scaling that discovery to the rest of the firm.
- A CEO using AI to draft internal strategy docs, modeling "safe experimentation" for the employees.
