# Augmentation vs. Automation

## Definition
A fundamental strategic choice in organizational design: using technology to replace human labor (automation) versus using it to enable humans to perform new, previously impossible, or higher-value tasks (augmentation).

## Why It Matters
Generative AI offers productivity gains that make automation tempting (e.g., cutting 30% of the workforce for a 30% gain). However, automation is a "simple arithmetic" that focuses on cost-cutting, whereas augmentation focuses on reimagining what an organization can *become* when its people are radically more capable.

## Key Arguments
- **Imagination vs. Arithmetic**: Automation is the default choice because it fits traditional financial models. Augmentation requires "genuine imagination" to see new products and markets.
- **The Scale of Capability**: Augmentation asks: "What does it mean if a single programmer can now write a hundred times more code?"
- **Institutional Knowledge**: Augmentation builds internal muscle and learning; automation often just cuts it.

## Examples
- **Automation**: Using AI to replace junior writers for routine copy.
- **Augmentation**: Enabling an existing writer to research and synthesize ten times as much material, creating deeper and more complex stories.
