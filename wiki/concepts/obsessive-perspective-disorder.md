# Obsessive Perspective Disorder (OPD)

## Definition
A psychological condition (coined by [[zadie-smith]]) found specifically in "Micro-Manager" writers during the first twenty pages of a novel. It is characterized by an existential drama over "What kind of a novel am I writing?" manifested through compulsive switching of voice and tone.

## Why It Matters
OPD shows that the difficulty of starting a long project is often a search for a "tonal frequency." Once the frequency is found, the rest of the project can proceed at "crazy speed."

## Key Arguments
- **Wait for Tone**: Smith argues that "finding the structure, its plot, its characters" are for many writers "contained in the sensibility of a sentence."
- **Incarceration**: Successful writers often view their first twenty pages as a "cell in which I was once incarcerated" due to the extreme worry and stilted verbiage.
- **The Toy Car Analogy**: Worrying over the start is like "winding the key of a toy car tighter and tighter" until the momentum is sufficient to finish the book.

## Examples
- **On Beauty**: Smith spent two years on the first twenty pages, then finished the rest in five months once the "tone" was set.
- **Micro-Managers**: Those who cannot proceed until the current "floor" is fully decorated and sturdy.
