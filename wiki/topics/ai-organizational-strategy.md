# AI Organizational Strategy

## What's the Story
The central corporate conflict of our time: how to integrate generative AI into large organizations without neutralizing its creative "weirdness." While efficiency dictates a "de-weirding" approach (treating AI as standard IT), true strategic advantage lies in augmentation and decentralizing experimentation.

## Key Arguments
- **The "IT Trap"**: Handing AI to IT departments leads to risk-aversion and stagnation.
- **The Information Gap**: Employees hide their AI use from managers if they don't feel the benefits are shared, creating a blind spot for leadership.
- **Normalization vs. Innovation**: Corporate normalization ("de-weirding") leads to automation and layoffs; confronting the "weirdness" leads to augmentation and growth.

## Timeline
- **2023-2024**: Initial "weirdness" of ChatGPT creates rapid bottom-up adoption.
- **2025-2026**: Counter-movement toward "de-weirding" as firms try to centralize and control AI usage (the Mollick critique).
