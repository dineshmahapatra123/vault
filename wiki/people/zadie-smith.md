# Zadie Smith

## Role/Background
Zadie Smith (born 1975) is an acclaimed British-Jamaican novelist, essayist, and short story writer. She rose to prominence with her debut novel *White Teeth* (2000) and is known for her insightful explorations of race, class, and the creative process.

## Known For
- **Literary Excellence**: Multiple award-winning novels including *White Teeth*, *The Autograph Man*, and *On Beauty*.
- **The "Micro Manager" Approach**: Her self-described methodical, sentence-by-sentence approach to novel construction, detailed in her essay *That Crafty Feeling* (2008); compiled into [[macro-planner-vs-micro-manager]].
- **Craft Criticism**: One of the few contemporary novelists who writes with "quasi-honest" transparency about the banal and fraudulent feelings associated with literary craft.

## Notable Pieces
- [[the-writing-process]] — Her definitive lecture (originally titled *"That Crafty Feeling"*) on the psychological stages of writing a novel.
- [[macro-planner-vs-micro-manager]] — The core framework introduced in the essay.
- [[obsessive-perspective-disorder]] — Her diagnosis of the beginning stage of a novel.

## Their Lens
Smith views the novel not as a pre-planned structure but as a "private language" that must be discovered through the act of writing itself. She is deeply suspicious of abstract craft talk, preferring to see each book as its own "independent republic" with its own set of rules.
