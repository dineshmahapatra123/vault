# Ethan Mollick

## Role/Background
Associate Professor at the Wharton School of the University of Pennsylvania. He is a leading researcher on innovation and entrepreneurship, with a recent focus on the organizational and individual impact of generative AI.

## Known For
- **Practical AI Implementation**: Arguing for a hands-on, experimental approach to using LLMs.
- **AI as "Weird"**: Promoting the idea that AI is a fundamentally different technology that shouldn't be treated as standard enterprise software.
- **Leadership-Crowd-Lab Model**: A framework for how organizations should adopt and scale AI.

## Notable Pieces
- *The IT Department: Where AI Goes to Die* (Economist, 2026) — compiled into [[de-weirding-ai]], [[augmentation-vs-automation]], [[leadership-crowd-lab-model]]
- *Co-Intelligence: Living and Working with AI* (2024)

## Their Lens
Mollick sees AI as a "weird", unpredictable collaborator rather than a predictable tool. He argues that trying to "normalize" or "de-weird" AI (fitting it into old IT management paradigms) is a strategic failure that leads to stagnation. For him, the creative and transformative power of AI comes from its "edges"—the unexpected things it can do that were previously impossible.
