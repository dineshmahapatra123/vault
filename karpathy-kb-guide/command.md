# KB Setup: Quick Reference

This is your "one-page" cheat sheet for using the Karpathy Knowledge Base. Save this in your Obsidian favorites.

---

### 1. The "Wake Up" Command
When you open a new chat (even months from now), type this to "re-index" your brain:

> **"Read @[/compile-kb] and do the needful for raw/[folder]/[filename].md"**

---

### 2. What I Do Automatically
Once you give that command, I follow this sequence without any further explanation needed:
- **I read the Workflow**: I look at `.agents/workflows/compile-kb.md` to remember *how* to build a Karpathy KB.
- **I read the Schema**: I look at `wiki/SCHEMA.md` to remember the *rules* for your articles.
- **I read the Index**: I look at `wiki/index.md` to see *what you already know* (so I don't create duplicates and can make new backlinks).
- **I read the Raw File**: I fetch your new reading and compile it into a multi-article set.

---

### 3. Your "Action" Workflow
To keep the system moving, your personal routine is just this:
1. **Ingest**: Drop your clipped article, paper, or README into the `raw/` folders.
2. **Command**: Tell me to `/compile-kb`.
3. **Reflect**: Open the `raw/notes/on-[file].md` template I created for you and write 2-3 sentences of your own thoughts.

---

### 💡 The Golden Rule: **The Files Are the Memory**
If you ever feel I am "drifting" or forgetting how you like things:

- **To change the rules**: Edit `wiki/SCHEMA.md`. It's my permanent "instruction manual."
- **To change the process**: Edit `WORKFLOW.md`. It's my "Standard Operating Procedure."

---

**You are now fully set up.** Your Knowledge Base has 15 articles, a clear structure, a working "voice" system, and a one-command automation.

**Go explore your new Wiki in Obsidian!**
