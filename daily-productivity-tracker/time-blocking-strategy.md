# Time Blocking Strategy

An innovative approach to allocating time throughout the workday. We don't just schedule tasks; we schedule cognitive states.

## The 4-Phase Day

Instead of a flat to-do list, time is partitioned into four distinct phases:

### 1. The Maker Phase (08:30 - 12:00)
- **Rules:** No meetings, no slack, no email. Phone on DND.
- **Goal:** Uninterrupted deep work on the single most important task of the day.
- **Role of [[assistant]]:** Intercepts all urgent requests.

### 2. The Manager Phase (13:00 - 14:30)
- **Rules:** Rapid fire context switching allowed.
- **Goal:** Unblock the team, answer emails, review documents, and handle logistics.

### 3. The Collaboration Phase (14:30 - 16:30)
- **Rules:** Synchronous communication only.
- **Goal:** 1-on-1s, team meetings, brainstorming sessions. This is where we align with the [[manager]].

### 4. The Shutdown Phase (16:30 - 17:00)
- **Rules:** Closing loops.
- **Goal:** Plan the Maker Phase for tomorrow. Update the [[daily-log]]. Shut down the laptop with zero cognitive residue.
