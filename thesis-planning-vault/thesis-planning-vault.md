# Thesis Planning

Use this page as the control center for topic definition, literature capture, and writing milestones.

## How To Use This Workspace

1. Capture possible angles, chapter ideas, or research directions in [[topics]].
2. Save every useful paper, book, article, or video in [[sources-and-notes]].
3. Convert deadlines, draft checkpoints, and submission milestones into [[timeline]].
4. Revisit this page weekly and update the thesis brief.

---

## Research Areas

Use [[topics]] to pressure-test ideas before they become chapters or major sections. A topic is worth keeping only if it is both interesting and workable.

## Literature And Notes

Use [[sources-and-notes]] as the evidence base for the thesis. Each entry should answer: what is this source arguing, and where will I use it?

## Writing And Milestones

Use the [[timeline]] for proposal deadlines, reading targets, chapter drafts, advisor reviews, revisions, and submission. If a milestone does not have a date, it is not planned.

## Advisor And Review Notes

- Add meeting outcomes here after each advisor conversation.
- Track decisions made, open questions, and required revisions.
- Link back to the relevant item in [[topics]], [[sources-and-notes]], or [[timeline]] when useful.

## Definition Of Done

- The research question is stable.
- Core literature is captured and tagged.
- Each chapter or section has a planned drafting window.
- Major institutional deadlines are in the timeline.
- The next writing task is always obvious.
